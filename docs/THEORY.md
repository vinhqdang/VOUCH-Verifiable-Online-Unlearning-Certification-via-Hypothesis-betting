## Theorem 1 (Exact, distribution-free null)

coin b_i given the pair (c_i‚Āį, c_i¬Ļ) ‚ÄĒ in particular under exact unlearning or

    D_i^(s) | F_{i‚ąí1}  is symmetric about 0,  hence  Z_i^(s) | F_{i‚ąí1} ~ Bern(1/2).

**Proof.** Exchangeability of (c‚Āį, c¬Ļ) means the joint law of
(c‚Āį, c¬Ļ, M_u) equals that of (c¬Ļ, c‚Āį, M_u) once M_u ‚üā b | pair, because the
pipeline saw either both twins symmetrically (never) or only the in-twin,
whose identity is determined by b, and b ‚üā everything. Swapping the twins
negates D while preserving its law, so D ‚Čź ‚ąíD. The tie-break coin makes the
sign exactly fair even with atoms at 0. ‚ąé

Consequences: every process built below is an exact e-process under the
success null with **no assumptions on the model, the score, or the data
distribution** ‚ÄĒ the null is generated by the verifier's own coins.

## Proposition 6 (VOUCH+ magnitude arm is exact)

Let g_i ‚ąą [0, 1] be any F_{i‚ąí1} ‚ą® ŌÉ(|D_i|)-measurable stake and
S_i = sign(D_i) (ties randomized). Under Theorem 1's null,
E[S_i | |D_i|, F_{i‚ąí1}] = 0, so E_t = ő†(1 + őĽ_i S_i g_i) with predictable
őĽ_i ‚ąą [0, 1) is an exact e-process. Choosing g_i as the predictable rank of

which is where sparse memorization lives; empirically this halves detection
time under 5%-sparse leakage. ‚ąé

## Proposition 7 (Honest limits)

(i) A VOUCH certificate is a statement about extractable influence through
F on the canary law; it does not imply parameter-space erasure (weight-space
information invisible to F is untouched ‚ÄĒ consistent with the behavioral
non-identifiability results cited in the design doc). (ii) With F closed
under negation (I4) the certificate excludes *both* directions of
score-detectable membership, but F remains finite; a score outside its span
can in principle leak. (iii) Canary‚Üíorganic transfer is calibrated, not
proven: the repetition strata give a measured dose‚Äďresponse curve őĒ(r), and
certificates are reported per stratum. ‚ąé

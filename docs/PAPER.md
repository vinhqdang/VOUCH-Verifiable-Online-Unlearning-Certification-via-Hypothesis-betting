# VOUCH: Anytime-Valid Certificates for Machine Unlearning

*Draft manuscript skeleton for Springer Machine Learning (alt: JMLR, TMLR).*
*Builds on the design document `algorithm.md` (v1.0); incorporates the corrections
and extensions I1–I4 (see `README.md`) with the empirical study in `results/`.*

## Abstract

Machine unlearning methods for LLMs ship with no statistical certificate that
forgetting occurred. Fixed-sample evaluations (TOFU forget-quality, MUSE PrivLeak,
MIA panels) are unsound as membership evidence without controlled randomness, break
under optional stopping and streaming deletions, and require retrained reference
models that are unaffordable and ill-defined at scale. We introduce VOUCH, the first
anytime-valid statistical certification framework for machine unlearning. The
provider plants paired ghost canaries — exchangeable twins, one trained-then-
forgotten, one never seen, assignment by fair coin, manifest hash-committed before
unlearning. A verifier then runs betting e-processes over the canary pairs: under
exact unlearning, within-pair score differences are symmetric *by construction*,
yielding an exact, distribution-free, finite-sample null with no shadow models and
no distributional assumptions. VOUCH outputs (i) an anytime-valid confidence
sequence on the residual membership advantage, (ii) an (ε, α)-Forgetting
Certificate via sequential equivalence testing — **two-sided**, since we find
empirically that gradient-ascent-style unlearning *over-forgets*, pushing forgotten
data to detectably bad scores, which is itself membership leakage — and (iii) a
dual revocation alarm. Certificates and alarms compose across streaming deletion
waves in *opposite* directions (all-pass for certificates, products for alarms),
which we show corrects an error in the natural "e-values multiply" composition:
the product of certificate e-values falsely "certifies" histories containing a bad
wave with probability → 1. Validity is exact at any stopping time (Ville);
power matches the KL information bound within 15%. We validate on 2,000-seed
simulations and end-to-end on GPT-2: retrain-from-scratch earns the certificate
(3/3 seeds), un-unlearned models are revoked within ~11 canary pairs, and the
revocation arm caught a sign bug in our own NPO implementation — illustrating that
certification also audits the unlearning *code*. A magnitude-aware revocation arm
(exact under the symmetry null) detects sparse memorization 2.3× faster than
sign-only betting. R-VOUCH re-certifies after relearning, 4-bit quantization, and
jailbreak-prompt probes, converting the "unlearning is suppression" critique into
a measured certificate clause.

## 1. Contributions

1. **Formulation.** Unlearning certification as sequential *two-sided* equivalence
   testing of the residual membership advantage, with an exact distribution-free
   null generated by the verifier's own randomness (paired ghost canaries routed
   through the full unlearning pipeline).
2. **Protocol.** Three coupled anytime-valid processes (certificate, confidence
   sequence, revocation) with sound multi-score composition (per-score min — we
   prove and measure that the natural adaptive-mixture certificate is anytime-
   invalid, 99.6% false certification), plus magnitude-aware betting (VOUCH+).
3. **Streaming theory.** Corrected composition across deletion waves: all-pass for
   certificates (union null), products for alarms (intersection null), α-spending
   for per-wave alarms; the product alarm provably and empirically accumulates
   distributed sub-threshold leakage invisible to every per-wave test.
4. **Theory.** Exact validity (Thm 1–2), power matching the KL bound (Thm 3, with
   corrected sample-size guidance), DP-bridge (Thm 4), composition (Thm 5′),
   exactness of the magnitude arm (Prop 6). See `docs/THEORY.md`.
5. **Empirics.** 2,000-seed validity/power/streaming study under adversarial
   optional stopping; end-to-end certification of GA/GradDiff/NPO/retrain on GPT-2
   (Colab T4) and a small-LM tier with affordable retrain ground truth; R-VOUCH
   probe study; released canary generator + verifier.

## 2. Method summary

(See `algorithm.md` §2–4 for the full setting; `vouch/` implements it.)

- **Phase 0.** Generate m twin pairs per deletion wave; coins b_i ~ Bern(1/2);
  in-twins inserted with repetition strata r ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8}; manifest SHA-256
  committed before unlearning.
- **Phase 1.** Unlearning runs with in-twins appended to the forget set.
- **Phase 2.** For each pair (random order): compute score differences
  D^s = s(M_u, c_in) − s(M_u, c_ghost) for s ∈ F = {loss, min-k, ratio(, probe)};
  signs feed per-score two-sided certificate e-processes (mixture bets:
  λ-grid + ONS + truncated-KT), the WSR confidence sequence, and the revocation
  arm (sign-mixture across scores + magnitude-aware symmetry e-process, both
  directions). Certificate ⇔ min over all per-score processes ≥ 1/α and all
  R-VOUCH probes pass; revocation ⇔ the revocation e-process ≥ 1/α.
- **Streaming.** Wave k alarm at α·2^−(k+1); global product alarm at α; global
  certificate = all waves pass.

## 3. Key experimental results

### 3.1 Validity under adversarial optional stopping (M1)

2,000 seeds, peek after every pair. VOUCH: false certification 0.028 / false
revocation 0.026 at α = 0.05 (0.006 / 0.006 at α = 0.01). Fixed-n binomial used
sequentially: 0.352 / 0.340 (0.119 / 0.098) — a 7× inflation that VOUCH eliminates
by construction. Full-protocol CS coverage 0.971 (nominal 0.95).

### 3.2 Soundness of multi-score composition (I1)

Composite null with one leaking score among three: design-doc mixture arm falsely
certifies 99.6% of runs; per-score min: 0.7% ≤ α.

### 3.3 Power (M2)

Median pairs to certification under exact unlearning (α = 0.05): 166 (ε = 0.2),
686 (ε = 0.1), 2,998 (ε = 0.05) — versus KL limits 147 / 596 / 2,394. Two-sided:
283 / 1,182 at ε = 0.2 / 0.1. Revocation detection: 48 pairs at Δ = 0.4, 690 at
Δ = 0.1. Magnitude arm: 2.3× faster under 5%-sparse strong leakage.

### 3.4 Streaming (M6)

10-wave histories: clean → per-wave FWER 0.000, global false alarm 0.002;
one bad wave (Δ = 0.25) → caught 0.984, never falsely certified globally;
ten weak waves (Δ = 0.06) → per-wave alarms 0.136, global product alarm 0.616
(median firing at wave 3), global certificate never issued.

### 3.5 End-to-end on GPT-2 (Colab T4; 3 seeds; 512 pairs; ε = 0.2)

| subject | status (3 seeds) | mean CS upper bound on Δ | mean D_loss |
|---|---|---|---|
| no unlearning | REVOKED ×3 (t ≈ 11) | 1.00 | 2.71 |
| retrain (exact) | **ISSUED ×3** | 0.22 | 0.01 |
| GA | REVOKED ×2, ISSUED ×1 | 0.45 | 0.13 |
| GradDiff | ISSUED ×2, UNDET ×1 | 0.23 | 0.12 |
| NPO (sign-bugged impl.) | REVOKED ×3 (t ≈ 11) | 1.00 | 2.74 |
| NPO + P1/P2/P3 probes | REVOKED ×3 | 1.00 | 2.2–2.8 |

The bugged-NPO row is deliberate to report: our first NPO implementation
minimized toward *memorization* (sign error); the revocation arm flagged every
run within ~11 pairs. Statistical certification audits not only the unlearning
outcome but the unlearning *implementation*. (v2 results with the corrected NPO,
two-sided certificates, and the ratio score: `results/lm_e2e_gpt2.json`.)

GradDiff also exhibited *over-forgetting* (mean D_loss = −0.155 on seed 0's
one-sided run) — in-twins scoring *below* ghosts — motivating the two-sided
certificate (I4).

### 3.6 Compute (M5)

VOUCH verification is pure inference: m × 2 × Q (× 2 with base-calibrated ratio)
short forward passes — ~10k forwards for m = 640, versus ≥ 16 full fine-tunes for
a U-LiRA shadow farm or one retrain for TOFU-style reference tests, i.e. two to
three orders of magnitude cheaper at equal or better error control.

## 4. Limitations

Relative to the declared score class and canary law (dose–response strata
calibrate, not prove, canary→organic transfer); honest-but-verifiable provider
(cryptographic attestation is orthogonal); canaries must be planted at
fine-tuning time; population-level (per-cohort) rather than per-user guarantees.

## 5. Reproducibility

All code, results, and figures in this repository; simulation suite ~15 min on
4 CPU cores; GPT-2 tier ~16 min/seed on one T4; unit tests validate the
supermartingale property, Ville bounds, CS coverage, and tamper detection.
